# Use Cases

Kanall is domain-blind — it does not know what a distributor, driver, student, or vendor is. It provisions accounts, records payments, and tracks ownership. What those things mean is entirely your business logic.

---

## FMCG and trade distribution

Hundreds of distributors paying into a shared account means daily manual matching. With Kanall, each distributor gets a dedicated NUBAN. When they pay an invoice, the payment lands in their account and a webhook fires instantly — invoice cleared, next delivery approved.

**Who this fits:** Distributors, FMCG manufacturers, pharmaceutical wholesalers, agri-supply chains.

---

## Logistics and freight

Collections from drivers or owner-operators get lost in a shared settlement pool. With Kanall, each driver gets their own account. Payments land with full attribution and weekly payouts settle directly from the confirmed ledger.

**Who this fits:** Trucking platforms, courier networks, last-mile delivery, freight aggregators.

---

## Gig economy and marketplaces

Matching buyer payments to the right service provider across a large pool is a reconciliation nightmare. With Kanall, each provider has a virtual account. Earnings land directly, balances are visible in real time, and payouts initiate from confirmed funds.

**Who this fits:** Freelance marketplaces, on-demand service platforms, creator payout systems.

---

## E-commerce checkouts

For one-off order payments, Kanall's one-time accounts let you issue a dedicated NUBAN per checkout. The account closes automatically once the exact payment arrives — no open window, no payment mismatch.

**Who this fits:** E-commerce stores, invoice-based businesses, ticketing platforms, order payment flows.

---

## School fee collection

Thousands of students paying through a shared account means daily manual matching to student records. With Kanall, each student gets a dedicated NUBAN. Fee payments land attributed, and your system marks the term paid the moment the webhook fires.

**Who this fits:** Schools, universities, EdTech platforms, exam-fee collection systems.

---

## Savings groups and cooperatives

Monthly member contributions come in through one account and someone reconciles manually every cycle. With Kanall, each member or group gets a virtual account. Contributions land attributed, balances are visible in real time, and payouts go out when the cycle closes.

**Who this fits:** Cooperative societies, ajo/esusu platforms, group savings apps.

---

## Rental and subscription billing

Rent or lease payments from multiple tenants land in one pool and arrears are tracked in a spreadsheet. With Kanall, each tenant gets a dedicated account. When payment arrives, the ledger records it and your platform marks the lease current — late payers have a visible gap.

**Who this fits:** Property managers, car rental fleets, equipment leasing, SaaS billing platforms.

---

## The pattern

Every use case above shares the same structure:

1. **One entity → one virtual account** — provision a NUBAN per entity at onboarding
2. **Payments arrive with attribution** — no matching, no reference codes
3. **The ledger is the truth** — confirmed balances are auditable and never lie
4. **Payouts from the ledger** — settle to any Nigerian bank account when ready

The domain changes. The primitive does not.
